Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist For College Students in India

Things You Should Do

1) Use unique passwords for all your accounts. This means using a mix of letters, numbers and symbols for each account.
2) Enable Two-Factor Authentication on your email, banking and social media accounts.
3) Verify links before you click on them links in emails, SMS or WhatsApp messages.
4) Keep your devices updated with the security patches and antivirus software.
5) Only use Wi-Fi networks and avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi.
6) Regularly check your bank statements and UPI transaction history to catch any activity.
7) Lock your phone with a PIN, fingerprint or face unlock.
8) Download apps from official sources like the Google Play Store or App Store.


Things You Should Not Do

1) Never share your OTP, PIN, CVV or passwords with anyone even if they claim to be from a bank.
2) Do not click on shortened links that claim you have won a prize need to refund something or need to take urgent action.
3) Avoid sharing much personal information on social media like your address, phone number or college ID.
4) Do not install apps from websites or APK files.
5) Never trust calls that ask you to update your KYC or verify your account urgently.
5) Do not use the password for multiple accounts.


UPI And Financial Safety Tips

1) Always verify the receivers name before sending them money via UPI.
2) Remember that receiving money does not require you to enter a PIN. If you are entering a PIN it means you are paying.
3) Avoid scanning QR codes from sources or strangers.
4) Immediately report any transactions to your bank.


 Report Cybercrime

If you face any cyber fraud or suspicious activity:

1) You can report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal: [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
2) You can also call the Cybercrime Helpline: 1930


Quick Tip

*If something looks too good to be true it probably is.
