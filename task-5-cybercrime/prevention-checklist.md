# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist For College Students in India

## Things You Should Do

* Use unique passwords for all your accounts. This means using a mix of letters, numbers and symbols for each account.

* Enable Two-Factor Authentication on your email, banking and social media accounts.

* Verify links before you click on them links in emails, SMS or WhatsApp messages.

* Keep your devices updated with the security patches and antivirus software.

* Only use Wi-Fi networks and avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi.

* Regularly check your bank statements and UPI transaction history to catch any activity.

* Lock your phone with a PIN, fingerprint or face unlock.

* Download apps from official sources like the Google Play Store or App Store.

---

## Things You Should Not Do

* Never share your OTP, PIN, CVV or passwords with anyone even if they claim to be from a bank.

* Do not click on shortened links that claim you have won a prize need to refund something or need to take urgent action.

* Avoid sharing much personal information on social media like your address, phone number or college ID.

* Do not install apps from websites or APK files.

* Never trust calls that ask you to update your KYC or verify your account urgently.

* Do not use the password for multiple accounts.

---

## UPI And Financial Safety Tips

* Always verify the receivers name before sending them money via UPI.

* Remember that receiving money does not require you to enter a PIN. If you are entering a PIN it means you are paying.

* Avoid scanning QR codes from sources or strangers.

* Immediately report any transactions to your bank.

---

## Report Cybercrime

If you face any cyber fraud or suspicious activity:

* You can report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal: [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)

* You can also call the Cybercrime Helpline: 1930
